why your smartphone is making you weak

01-07-2024

let me guess - you checked your phone at least 10 times while reading this sentence. don't worry, i won't judge... much. 😏

as an engineer who designs complex systems, i find it fascinating how we've become the most sophisticated machines ever designed... only to be defeated by notification dopamine hits.

marcus aurelius never had to deal with instagram thirst traps or linkedin humble brags. lucky bastard. but his principles are more relevant now than ever:

"very little is needed to make a happy life; it is all within yourself, in your way of thinking."

spoiler alert: that 'very little' doesn't include your 47 daily tiktok dance videos.

here's what your phone is actually doing to you:

1. destroying your attention span
remember when you could read a book without checking twitter every 3 minutes? yeah, me neither.

2. weakening your mental resilience
why solve a problem when you can scroll through memes about having problems?

3. killing your creativity
leonardo da vinci didn't have netflix. just saying.

the solution? i'm not suggesting you become a digital hermit (though that would make for an interesting linkedin post). instead:

- set boundaries like they're restraining orders
- treat notifications like they're your clingy ex
- make your phone earn its place in your life

remember: you're the descendant of warriors, philosophers, and innovators. act like it.

now excuse me while i post this on my smartphone. oh, the irony... 🤔
