about

i was trained as an engineer. that gave me a way of thinking: break things down, understand the structure, respect reality, build from first principles.

but i was never meant for one lane alone.

my attention moved beyond how things work, into how they are directed, shaped, and carried through. engineering gave me structure. management gave me movement. culture gave me a reason to care.

today, i move between engineering, management, and creation. i am drawn to systems, good work, and ideas that deserve a stronger form. i care about clarity, discipline, aesthetics, and the difference between something that works and something that lasts.

i do not see disciplines as separate identities. engineering, design, leadership, storytelling, and culture belong closer together than most people think.

i try to keep mind and body under the same discipline. not as a habit. as a standard.
(i have to work on the enough sleep part.)

the things i build are not separate from who i am becoming.

the goal is not to know more.

the goal is to build better.

still in progress.
